Civil & Structural Engineering

Civil & Structural Engineering involves designing, constructing, and maintaining infrastructure such as buildings, bridges, roads, and dams. These fields are essential for urban development, ensuring safety, sustainability, and functionality. Freshers earn ₹3–8 lakh, mid-level professionals ₹8–20 lakh, and experienced professionals ₹20–50 lakh. Senior roles can exceed ₹1 crore annually.

Available Roles

Structural Engineer

Design and analyze structures to ensure they can withstand loads and stresses.

Civil Engineer

Plan, design, and oversee construction projects such as roads, bridges, and water systems.

Construction Manager

Supervise construction projects, ensuring they are completed on time and within budget.

Geotechnical Engineer

Study soil and rock properties to assess suitability for construction projects.

Transportation Engineer

Design and maintain transportation systems, including roads, highways, and railways.

Environmental Engineer

Develop solutions to environmental challenges, such as waste management and pollution control.

Project Engineer

Manage technical aspects of construction projects, ensuring compliance with plans and specifications.

BIM Specialist

Create and manage digital models of construction projects using Building Information Modeling (BIM).

Bridge Engineer

Specialize in designing and maintaining bridges, ensuring structural integrity and safety.

Urban Planner

Develop plans for land use in urban areas, balancing development with community needs.

Water Resources Engineer

Design systems for managing water resources, such as dams, reservoirs, and irrigation networks.

Site Engineer

Oversee day-to-day operations at construction sites, ensuring quality and safety standards are met.
