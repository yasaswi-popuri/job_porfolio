Supply Chain & Inventory Management Field

Supply Chain & Inventory Management involves planning, implementing, and controlling the flow of goods, services, and information from production to consumption. These fields are critical for ensuring efficiency, reducing costs, and meeting customer demands. Freshers earn ₹3–8 lakh, mid-level ₹8–15 lakh, and experienced professionals ₹15–30 lakh. Senior roles can exceed ₹50 lakh annually.

Available Roles

Supply Chain Analyst

Analyze supply chain processes and recommend improvements for efficiency.

Inventory Manager

Oversee inventory levels, track stock movements, and ensure timely replenishment.

Logistics Coordinator

Manage transportation, warehousing, and delivery of goods to meet deadlines.

Purchasing Manager

Source and negotiate with suppliers to procure materials and products at optimal costs.

Operations Manager

Oversee daily operations to ensure smooth functioning of supply chain activities.

Demand Planner

Forecast demand and plan inventory to meet customer needs effectively.

Procurement Specialist

Develop strategies for sourcing materials and managing supplier relationships.

Warehouse Supervisor

Manage warehouse operations, including storage, safety, and staff coordination.

Supply Chain Consultant

Advise organizations on optimizing their supply chain processes and strategies.

Transportation Manager

Plan and oversee the movement of goods via road, rail, air, or sea.

Inventory Control Specialist

Implement systems to track and manage inventory accuracy and efficiency.

Vendor Relationship Manager

Maintain strong relationships with suppliers and vendors to ensure timely deliveries.
