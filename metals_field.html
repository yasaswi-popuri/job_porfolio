Materials & Metallurgical Engineering Field

Materials & Metallurgical Engineering involves the study, development, and application of materials such as metals, alloys, ceramics, polymers, and composites. These fields are critical for industries like aerospace, automotive, construction, and manufacturing. Freshers earn ₹3–8 lakh, mid-level ₹8–15 lakh, and experienced professionals ₹15–30 lakh. Senior roles can exceed ₹50 lakh annually.

Available Roles

Metallurgist

Study the properties and behavior of metals and alloys for industrial applications.

Materials Engineer

Develop and test new materials for use in various industries.

Quality Control Inspector

Ensure materials and products meet quality standards and specifications.

Process Engineer

Optimize manufacturing processes for materials production.

Research Scientist

Conduct research on advanced materials and their applications.

Failure Analyst

Investigate material failures and recommend improvements.

Corrosion Engineer

Prevent and mitigate corrosion in materials and structures.

Welding Engineer

Design and oversee welding processes for metal fabrication.

Ceramics Engineer

Develop ceramic materials for electronics, aerospace, and other industries.

Polymer Engineer

Work with plastics and polymers for product development and manufacturing.

Composite Materials Specialist

Develop lightweight and durable composite materials for aerospace and automotive industries.

Manufacturing Engineer

Optimize the production of materials and components in manufacturing plants.
