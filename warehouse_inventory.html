Supply Chain Management Field

Supply Chain Management involves planning, implementing, and controlling the flow of goods, services, and information from production to consumption. It ensures efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and customer satisfaction. Freshers earn ₹3–8 lakh, mid-level professionals ₹8–15 lakh, and experienced individuals ₹15–30 lakh annually. Senior roles can exceed ₹50 lakh.

Available Roles

Supply Chain Analyst

Analyze supply chain data to optimize processes, reduce costs, and improve efficiency.

Procurement Manager

Oversee the purchasing of goods and services, ensuring quality, cost-effectiveness, and timely delivery.

Logistics Coordinator

Manage the transportation, storage, and distribution of goods across the supply chain.

Inventory Manager

Monitor inventory levels, forecast demand, and ensure optimal stock availability.

Warehouse Supervisor

Oversee warehouse operations, including receiving, storing, and dispatching goods.

Demand Planner

Predict future product demand to align production and procurement strategies.

Operations Manager

Ensure smooth operations across the supply chain, from manufacturing to delivery.

Supply Chain Consultant

Advise organizations on improving their supply chain processes and strategies.

Transportation Manager

Plan and coordinate the movement of goods via road, rail, air, or sea.

Quality Assurance Specialist

Ensure products meet quality standards throughout the supply chain process.

Sustainability Manager

Develop eco-friendly practices to reduce the environmental impact of supply chain operations.

Customer Service Representative

Address customer queries and resolve issues related to order fulfillment and delivery.
